My Education and Study Abroad Experience


During my first year at the University of Notre Dame, I experienced significant growth as a student, leader, and individual through my courses and activities. By including credits for studying abroad over the summer, I will be entering my second year with 44 credits. This diverse set of coursework has prepared me to problem solve, work as a team, and better understand engineering. In the summer after my first year, I participated in the engineering summer study abroad program in London. This six-week program allowed me to take two courses for six credits that fulfill university requirements in Central London. This was a program for 50 undergraduate engineers, and it was taught by 3 Notre Dame professors.


This program provided me the opportunitity to live and learn in one of the most international cities in the world. My education in London had provided me with a unique environment that had broaden my intellectual and cultural horizons. Classes are taught just off bustling Trafalgar Square in the heart of London. My courses included visiting museums and art galleries, theater performances, and historic sites. I also had the opportunity to travel independently. In the 6 weeks, I visited Cambridge, Oxford, Dublin, Galway, Aran Islands, Cliffs of Moher, Zurich, Milan, Greenwich, Seven Sisters Cliffs, and Brighton.


I am grateful for this experience, as it helped me grow as a person by expanding my perspective and introducing me to other engineers in the program. The program focused heavily on group work and presenting, which enhanced my collaboration and public speaking skills.

My Service: Fighting Hunger


In high school, I was an active member of Operation Others, an organization run by Catholic high school students in the Omaha area. Our mission was to provide food assistance to 1,200 families during the Christmas season, educate the community about food insecurity, and encourage a preferential option for the poor. Through my volunteering, I organized canned food drives, coordinated "Trunk or Treat" events at Catholic schools in Omaha, and assisted with packaging and truck deliveries.


At Notre Dame, I continued to volunteer in the food industry. Every Saturday in the fall, I volunteered with other members of my dorm at GreenBridge Growers, a farm that uses innovative methods to grow sustainable, fresh produce for the state of Indiana. My volunteering included picking fruits and vegetables, weeding, and maintaining the farm.
